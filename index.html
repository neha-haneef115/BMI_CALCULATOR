Q2. Why is BMI important?
Answer: BMI is a useful indicator of overall health and can help identify individuals at risk of developing conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, or hypertension. However, it should be considered alongside other health assessments.

Answer: BMI may not be equally accurate across all populations. For example, Asian populations may be at risk of health conditions at lower BMI thresholds, while athletes may need additional methods to assess body composition.
